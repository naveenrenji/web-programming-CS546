My Education

MS Ramaiah Institute of Technology

My favorite class was web programming, I learned all about NodeJS, MongoDB and ExpressJS. I also learned so much about frontend development like HTML, CSS and Bootstrap. The final project was so much fun, especially getting to work with my team and learn a lot about web technologies and team work.

My freshman year of college, I took night classes, lucking into a class with an English professor who I thought was just awesome—she was friendly, approachable, articulate and accomplished. She had experience writing for TV programs I actually recognized (afterschool specials, believe it or not). She made a great first impression and I looked forward to spending the next ten weeks, learning from her. That first class, we were given an assignment to write an essay. I don’t even remember what it was about, but I know I wasn’t worried about writing it. I turned it in during the next class and didn’t give it a second thought. As you’d expect, our graded papers were passed back to us during the next class. When one of my classmates got his paper, he started cursing. Loudly. “Who the f— does this lady [that’s not what he called her] think she is.” He proceeded to make nasty comments about her appearance, age, sex, intelligence…it was all fair game. She tried to get him to quiet down. He was having none of that. She eventually told him he would have to leave. “F— this place!” he screamed as he left the room.

Now, he left, but he didn’t go far. During that class, he paced the hall, cursing up a storm. Every so often, he’d glare into the classroom through the window in the door. When we had our first break, the professor asked one of the more muscular guys in the class to accompany her while she went to get security. Soon enough, there was a confrontation in the hallway, as that classmate started screaming at the security guard, at first refusing to leave, then threatening to sue him and the professor, yelling that he had rights, after all. I thought that would be the end of it, but I was wrong. As I was walking to my bus stop, he jumped out of the bushes that he was hiding in (I kid you not) and started trying to plead his case with me. “You’re in Professor Smith’s [not her real name] class, right?” I was panicking and my mind screamed at me to not engage with him. I tried to ignore him and act like I didn’t hear him. “I know you can hear me,” he said, and as I’m thinking ‘Crap, crap, crap, ’ he took out his essay and tried to shove it in my face. “Look at all these marks! Look! Look!”

I told him that, yes, I was in the class, but that I did not want to get involved. I inwardly flinched, expecting him to press the issue further, but in a tone that reminded me of a frustrated five-year-old, he said, “Fine,” then turned on his heel and walked away. Over the next few weeks, he proceeded to harass that professor. It was so bad, she had to have an escort to and from the classes, among other things. The saddest thing was how it changed her: She transformed from all that lovely confidence to a closed-off, frightened person who jumped any time a door shut. I wish I knew for sure what happened to her, but I don’t believe she ever taught at the university again. I heard that he was expelled and banned from ever being on the campus again. It was inferred that there had been previous issues. Given how creepy he was, I didn’t doubt it.
